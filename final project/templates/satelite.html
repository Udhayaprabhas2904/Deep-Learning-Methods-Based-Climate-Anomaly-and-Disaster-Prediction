Identify Core Visual Elements

To distinguish between different land features, use the seven primary elements of visual interpretation:


  • Tone/Color: Water is typically black or dark blue, while bare ground is tan or brown.

  • Shape:Look for straight lines and geometric shapes (rectangles, circles) which usually indicate human activity like farms or roads.

  • Size: Comparing the size of an object to known nearby landmarks helps determine its scale.

  • Pattern:Repetitive arrangements, such as the grid of a city or the rows in an orchard, provide clues to land use.

  • Texture:Forests often look "rough" or bumpy, while water or paved surfaces appear smooth.

  • Shadow:Shadows can reveal the height and shape of objects like buildings or mountains.

  • Association:Connecting a feature to its surroundings (e.g., a boat being next to a dock) helps confirm its identity.
